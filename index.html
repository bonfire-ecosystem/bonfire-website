Bonfire is a federated and modular social network. It can be assembled and configured from the bottom-up to empower individuals and communities to design, operate and control their own digital lives.

Built upon ActivityPub - a W3C standard protocol - every Bonfire instance can communicate with the rest of the so-called fediverse, a virtual decentralised social space where users of many different open source platforms can all interconnect. The beauty of the fediverse lies also in its diversity. From posts and articles, to photo and video, from audio to blogging to gathering the range of activities that can be conducted on the fediverse is constantly growing. With Bonfire we want to ensure that with the same login and without the need to check many different apps, a user can receive updates from all the users and topics they follow, independently from the platform where the activities are created.

We want to push the boundaries further, and develop plugins to implement a whole range of new use cases, for example to enable economic activities and community coordination - in collaboration with the ValueFlows and ForgeFed efforts.

We think mixing social and economic activities together in a federated social network can unlock a whole new range of unexplored scenarios and use cases that can empower solidarity networks: create offers and needs listings and geolocate them on a map; create, discuss and vote on proposals; coordinate labour and distribute outcomes among participants; create a timebanking app; create shared budgets or crowdfunding campaigns. Any of these scenarios can be well designed with a combination of economic and social features and flourish on a federated and open environment.

As activists in first place, we want to partner with communities, progressive municipalities, cooperatives and grassroots movements to design and build together such plugins and make them available for everyone to use, remix and experiment.
